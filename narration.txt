April first, twenty twenty-six. For the first time in over fifty years, humans are leaving Earth orbit. This is Artemis Two.

The Space Launch System carries four astronauts into a high elliptical parking orbit, twenty-four hours of system checkouts before committing to the Moon.

A six-minute engine burn — Trans-Lunar Injection — accelerates Orion by three kilometers per second onto a free-return trajectory.

Four days of outbound coast. Earth shrinks behind. The Moon grows ahead. No shortcuts, no reverse.

April sixth. Closest approach: six thousand five hundred kilometers above the lunar far side. Wiseman, Glover, Koch, Hansen — the first humans this far from home since Apollo.

Gravity brings them back. No burn required. The figure-eight completes itself. Re-entry velocity climbs toward eleven kilometers per second.

April eleventh. Splashdown in the Pacific. Ten days. One flyby. Four astronauts. Infinite ambition. Next: Artemis Three — boots on the Moon.
